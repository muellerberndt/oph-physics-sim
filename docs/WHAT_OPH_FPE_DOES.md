# OPH-FPE: finite experiments in observer-built physics

OPH-FPE turns the observer-consistency claims of Observer-Patch Holography into
finite runs that earn public receipts or expose a missing bridge. Its technical
differentiator is concrete: each experiment instantiates observer-like
self-reading systems.

A run opens on a finite screen in disagreement. Each patch owns bounded local
state, exposes boundary ports, reads its own state and shared interfaces, writes
records, and may change through feedback or repair. On one overlap, the two
sides fail to match after gauge transport. The program proposes a local repair,
measures the resulting mismatch, and accepts or rejects the move. Repeated
across the carrier, this process may settle into stable records, exhaust its run
budget, or reveal a failed invariant.

The declared inputs include a finite carrier, its connectivity, group-valued
boundary data, a seed, and a repair-and-test configuration. A conventional
cosmology code begins with spacetime, fields, particles, and evolution
equations. OPH-FPE asks an earlier question: can bounded systems with local
readback and repair support shared records from which observer-facing
structures can be reconstructed? The screen is a regulator input, so
“geometry from nothing” lies outside the claim.

```text
bounded patches -> local readback -> overlap repair -> committed records -> gated reconstruction
```

## What a run tests

After repair, separate analysis lanes ask increasingly demanding questions.
The finite-consensus lane checks descent, repair completeness, confluence,
schedule independence, and terminal normal forms. The observer lane checks
local modular time and agreement between different gauge views of one
committed shared record. The geometry lane keeps an observer-facing H3 chart
separate from a chart-blind neutral third-person bulk. The defect lane follows
holonomy clusters and worldlines, then tests the stronger conditions required
for particle promotion. The cosmology lane produces screen and freezeout
diagnostics, with physical-CMB promotion reserved for runs that satisfy the
source, transfer, and frozen-likelihood gates.

These lanes cannot substitute for one another. A chart receipt does not supply
a neutral bulk or an Einstein equation. A persistent defect track does not
supply a physical particle. A screen angular spectrum does not supply a
physical CMB prediction.

## What a run publishes

The main product is a public evidence bundle containing the exact configuration
and seed, simulator and research revisions, mismatch and repair traces,
committed records, controls, observer and geometry reports, defect and
cosmology outputs, hashes, and machine-readable receipts with blocker lists. A
false receipt is a valid result. It identifies the exact point where a proposed
emergence chain fails.

Browser-ready views consume the same bundle. Computed structures, explicit
assumptions used for explanatory rendering, and blocked interpretations carry
separate labels. An assumed de Sitter layer may complete a visual scene while
the Einstein and gravity receipts stay false. A defect may receive matter-like
visual styling while the particle receipt stays false. The evidence labels
travel with the data.

Paper proofs and finite receipts have separate jobs. The proof stack states the
structural or limiting claim. The simulator asks whether a declared finite
carrier instantiates the required hypotheses and survives the corresponding
controls.

## Claim boundary

The lattice’s scientific role is structural. Its relaxation dynamics does not
consume the OPH pixel constant P, and it produces no fine-structure constant,
scalar tilt, particle masses, or cosmological constant. Those quantities belong
to paper-side analytic claims and comparisons.

The package contains an exact 12-patch calibration verifier and larger finite
carrier experiments. Selected earned bundles carry positive, contract-scoped
receipts for observer-local time, controlled observer-facing H3 structure,
shared-record consistency, and proto-particle assays. Strict neutral bulk, the
full finite Lorentz contract, Einstein branch entry, production particles,
production gravity, and physical CMB carry false or open receipts.

The string-vacuum lane is an ingestion and verification contract. OPH-FPE has
no compactification, stabilization, massive-spectrum, or string-threshold
solver. `STRING_VACUUM_SELECTION_RECEIPT_CONTRACT.md` specifies the external
artifacts, augmented rank/isolation proof, branch coverage, and catalogue
evidence required for that lane. `STRING_VACUUM_SIMULATOR_TARGETS.md` fixes the
machine-readable observable values, receipt dependency ladder, failure scopes,
and replay targets. Its scientific promotion gates remain false.

OPH-FPE’s useful output is an auditable map from local observer rules to the
claims they support, with each missing bridge left visible.
