There are several cosmology directions where OPH could make genuinely distinctive predictions rather than simply rephrasing existing cosmology. I would prioritize them roughly as follows.

## 1. The CMB as an Observer-Consensus Fossil

This is probably the single highest-value cosmology project.

The key question is:

> If the bulk emerges from overlap-consistency among observer patches, what statistical imprint does that process leave on the largest observable scales?

Instead of treating the CMB as primordial quantum fluctuations from inflation, derive it as a relic of finite-capacity observer synchronization and bulk reconstruction.

Questions:

* Why is the spectrum nearly scale invariant?
* Why are there acoustic peaks?
* Why are the low multipoles slightly anomalous?
* Should there be parity asymmetries?
* Should there be preferred large-angle correlations?
* Are there observable signatures of finite screen capacity?

This is the most obvious bridge from OPH's screen-first ontology to data.

The screen-capacity branch already ties cosmological scales to finite horizon information content .

If yes, this becomes one of the most interesting OPH cosmology papers.
